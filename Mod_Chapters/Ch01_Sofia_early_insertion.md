# Ch01 — Early Sofia Voice Insertion

## Where to insert

After "Minutes passed." (line 28), before "Then she called out from behind the door..."

---

## Draft block

[Sofia]
I'd bought this three ports ago. Wrapped in tissue, tucked at the bottom of my case, waiting for a moment I wasn't sure would come.

After Santorini, I'd almost left it there. What's the point of lingerie when everything is broken?

But this morning — the warmth of him, my leg across his, the shower — I thought: maybe. Maybe we're not done. Maybe I can make him remember what this is.

I'm not sure if I was seducing him or reminding myself.

I stood in front of the narrow bathroom mirror and looked at myself in the turquoise lace and thought: whoever this woman is, she is not afraid.

I was afraid.

But she wasn't.

I took a breath.

Then I opened the door.
[/Sofia]

---

## Why this works

- Arrives before the sex — Sofia exists as a person before she becomes an object of desire
- Reframes the theatrical entrance: not performance for him, but something she needed
- "whoever this woman is, she is not afraid / I was afraid" plants the dissociation early — pays off in the bathroom mirror block ("Is that me?")
- Short enough not to break momentum, but changes what the reader brings to "close your eyes"
- Shifts her from respondent to agent — which is what the whole manuscript argues about her
