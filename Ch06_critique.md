## 4. Weaknesses — With Resolution Paths

**Weakness #1: Julia still has almost zero voice**

**Problem:** Julia speaks one line in the entire chapter: "You know, I've been told that before." (Line 68)

**Specificity of failure:**
- The Italian confession is paraphrased ("She said no the first night. But the second night, she said yes.")
- The Spaniard's filming is told ("He asked to film her. She said yes.") but not experienced
- The American confession is quoted: "I've just spent two days fucking the American." But there's no context, no emotional context, no Julia's state of mind—just the narrator's reaction to it

**Resolution path:** Give Julia dialogue in at least three moments:
1. The conversation with the Italian at dinner—let her say yes and explain why
2. The moment the Spaniard asks to film her—let her hesitate, decide, feel
3. The American texting the narrator—show Julia's tone, her intention. Is she confessing? Bragging? Punishing?

---

**Weakness #2: The hotel manager's racism is mentioned but not explored**

**Problem:** Lines 50–52: "The American defended her. But they left anyway... She arrived in Cyprus cold, distant, still soaked in the scent of him."

The hotel manager calls Julia a "Black whore." This is racist, violent, and traumatic. But it's mentioned in passing and never referenced again.

**Specificity of failure:**
- Is this a moment that hurt Julia? The narrator doesn't say.
- Does it affect how she feels about the American? No indication.
- Does the narrator recognize the racism as violence? No—he moves immediately to his own jealousy.

The racism becomes atmospheric detail rather than a moment of significance.

**Resolution path:** Explore this moment:
- Show Julia's reaction to being called a "Black whore"
- Show the American defending her (or not) and how that lands
- Show whether this moment is traumatic for Julia or empowering (she stands with the American against the hotel manager)
- Connect it to the broader pattern of Julia being desired/objectified/dehumanized

---

**Weakness #3: The narration of affairs is still summary, not full scene**

**Problem:** The Italian, Spaniard, and American sections are detailed but still told from the narrator's perspective in retrospect. The reader gets the information but not the experience.

**Specificity of failure:**
- Italian: "She said no the first night. But the second night, she said yes." — We don't see their conversation
- Spaniard: "They flirted over dinner... She told the Spaniard to take her back to his hotel." — We don't hear the flirtation
- American: "They fucked for four hours, pausing only for sensual showers..." — We hear the mechanics but not Julia's experience or choice-making in the moment

**Resolution path:** Expand at least one of these into a dramatized scene:
- Show Julia and the Italian at dinner. Show her hesitation and her choice. Show them in the hotel room.
- Or show Julia and the Spaniard. Show her telling him to take her back. Show them fucking, the filming request, her hesitation and agreement.
- Or show Julia and the American. Show their campus encounter. Show the Ericeira hotel, the two days, her choice to be there.

**Weakness #4: The chapter's ending teases content not delivered**

**Problem:** Lines 72–75: "The second came weeks later. In Athens. The infamous bloodbath... But we'll get to that."

**Specificity of failure:** The chapter ends with a promise of Athens, which has been referenced multiple times (Ch05 as "the Greek in Athens" with period blood, now as "the infamous bloodbath"). But we don't get it in this chapter. The reader is told to wait.

This works as foreshadowing, but it's also a cop-out. The chapter could end with Athens instead of teasing it.

**Problem:** Lines 60–75 shift into literary, self-aware voice:

**Resolution path:** Either:
